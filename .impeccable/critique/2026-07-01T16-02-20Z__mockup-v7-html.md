## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Does it look AI-generated? No.** This reads as a real, opinionated signage system — bottom-anchored cohesive block, one bronze direction, dark stage, purposeful motion. It avoids the "cluttered ad-board" and "generic-energetic gradient-startup" anti-references in PRODUCT.md.

**Deterministic scan (76 findings):**
- `design-system-color` (32) + `design-system-radius` (30): color/radius literals drift from DESIGN.md tokens. Radius is mostly a false-positive (kiosk is deliberately `vh`-based, not px tokens). Color is partly real — see #FBD7A8 below.
- `design-system-font` (5): Anton (the documented display font) is absent; Mitr is loaded but off-token. Real consistency drift.
- `bounce-easing` (1, line 50): `cubic-bezier(0.34,1.56,0.64,1)` overshoot. Real; against the skill's no-bounce rule.
- `layout-transition` (1, line 926): `transition: width` animates a layout property. Real but tiny.
- `broken-image` (7): **false positives** — these `<img>` (slot-media-img, qrImage, plogo, cat-card) are populated by JS at runtime; they render fine. The detector can't see JS.

## Overall Impression
A genuinely premium, lively lobby screen — it looks like "the nicest thing in the lobby," which is the brand's entire flywheel. The biggest opportunity is a single tension it just introduced: **text transparency now fights the brand's #1 principle, distance legibility.** Resolve that and tighten two identity drifts (gold, fonts) and this is excellent.

## What's Working
1. **Bilingual craft.** Thai line-break as nowrap word-units + author `|` hint + IBM Plex Sans Thai fallbacks everywhere. This is real, uncommon care and directly serves principle #3 (bilingual parity).
2. **Motion with a calm fallback.** Ken-Burns (cover-only), cross-dissolve, CTA shimmer/breathe — plus a global `prefers-reduced-motion` catch-all that neutralizes them all. On-brand "energy through motion," and the required reduced-motion alternative is actually present.
3. **Restraint & hierarchy.** One cohesive bottom-anchored block, generous dark space, single accent direction. "Energetic screen with taste," not a busy board.

## Priority Issues

- **[P1] Title at 65% opacity undercuts distance-legibility (the #1 brand principle).**
  Why it matters: PRODUCT.md principle #4 + accessibility priority #1 are both "distance-legible, high contrast, no thin grays." The title is the largest, most important element and was just set to `opacity:0.65` to let the photo through. The bottom scrim (`slot-grad-bot`) is strongest at the very bottom and fades upward — exactly where the title/eyebrow sit it's weakest — so a 65% title over a busy photo risks failing the from-across-the-room read that IS the product. (Deliberate user choice, but it collides with the stated priority.)
  Fix: keep the "photo reads through" intent but move the transparency off the text — e.g. raise the title toward 0.85–1.0 and instead add a localized scrim/blur *behind the text block* (a stronger, tighter gradient or a subtle backdrop panel) so the surround shows the photo while the text stays crisp.
  Suggested command: `/impeccable layout` (or a contrast pass)

- **[P2] Two golds dilute the single-bronze identity.**
  Why it matters: DESIGN.md says bronze `#C9864C` is the *single* accent (≤10%). Price + detail use `#FBD7A8` (a lighter warm gold, 7 occurrences) — on the near-black stage it reads as a second accent color. Price is a high-value element wearing an off-brand gold.
  Fix: decide one — use the brand bronze for price, OR (if bronze is too dark to read on near-black, which is likely why the lighter gold was chosen) formally adopt `#FBD7A8` as a documented "stage gold" token so it's intentional, not drift.
  Suggested command: `/impeccable colorize`

- **[P2] Font system doesn't match DESIGN.md.**
  Why it matters: DESIGN.md documents `display: Anton`, but Anton isn't loaded; the title is now Prompt (changed today), the logo is Mitr — three families with the documented display font absent. The design doc no longer describes reality.
  Fix: pick the intended display font (Prompt looks right after today's "thinner" change) and update DESIGN.md; drop Mitr if the logo can move to the chosen family.
  Suggested command: `/impeccable document` (regenerate DESIGN.md) then `/impeccable typeset`

- **[P3] Bounce/overshoot easing.**
  Why it matters: `cubic-bezier(0.34,1.56,0.64,1)` (line 50) overshoots; the skill bans bounce/elastic as dated. Brand energy should come from motion + bronze-on-navy, not spring bounce.
  Fix: swap to ease-out-quint/expo for that entrance.
  Suggested command: `/impeccable animate`

- **[P3] Minor perf/token drift.**
  `transition: width` (line 926) animates layout; `vh`-based radii diverge from the px token scale (intentional for the kiosk — low priority; consider documenting the kiosk's vh scale as its own token set).
  Suggested command: `/impeccable optimize`

## Persona Red Flags

**Lobby viewer (glancing from ~5 m, Thai-first):** the 65%-opacity title over a busy photo is the exact case they fail — can't read the offer from across the room. Price in a non-brand gold reads as "a different thing."

**Advertiser (shop owner seeing their own ad):** their headline rendered semi-transparent competes with the photo — may feel "my ad looks faded," the opposite of the premium placement they paid for.

**Staff (authoring in Sanity):** two non-obvious gotchas with no in-Studio guidance — the `|` line-break convention, and "the player shows *Image Files*, not the top poster." Both bit us in real use today; a field description in the schema would prevent repeats.

## Minor Observations
- Detail popup has no explicit loading state before media/provider data paints.
- `#FBD7A8` and the rgba literals could become 2–3 stage tokens to stop future drift.
- The kiosk's `vh` radius scale is coherent but undocumented — worth a note in DESIGN.md so it's not read as drift.

## Questions to Consider
- What if the photo read through the *surround* (a tighter scrim behind text) instead of through the letters — keeping the airy feel without softening the pitch?
- Is `#FBD7A8` a deliberate "stage-legible bronze," or accidental? If deliberate, name it; if not, unify to bronze.
- Should DESIGN.md's display font be Anton (as written) or Prompt (as shipped)? The doc should match the screen.
